# Vharos World Primer

## AT A GLANCE

Vharos (VAH-ross) is a jungle death world where altitude is civilization and lowland abundance is a trap. Fortress-cities cling to mountain stone, river bluffs, and old caldera rims while the canopy below consumes roads faster than masons can lay them. Trade exists, but only where escorts, weathercraft, and faction treaties keep caravans alive long enough to matter.

Ascendency has changed the balance of power. Individuals carrying awakened divine sparks now influence law, war, faith, and commerce as much as crowns and councils. In this age, survival is political, and politics is theological.

### Canon Constraints

- Vharos remains a high-fantasy 5e setting centered on lethal jungle survival.
- The opening campaign pressure is the fall crisis of Kharad-Vul and Underdark incursions.
- Ascendants are uncommon but increasingly decisive in regional power struggles.
- Institutions are practical first: food, roads, defense, salvage, and oath enforcement.

## TIME IN VHAROS

Vharos tracks civil life by practical labor cycles rather than abstract astronomy. Most city charters count days in ten-day turns called chains, grouped into thirteen floodmarks per year. In lowland communities, this calendar is often supplemented by rain signs, bloom cycles, and migratory beast patterns.

### Canon Facts

- The common civil unit is the ten-day chain, used for wages, rations, contracts, and caravan terms.
- The standard year is thirteen floodmarks of four chains each, plus three intercalary Ember Nights used for audits, amnesties, and treaty renewal.
- Kharad-Vul and affiliated highland holds ring shifts by bell watches, not sun position.
- Most legal deadlines are recorded as chain-and-bell, allowing precise enforcement in enclosed cities.

### Uncertain Reports

- Some chronologers claim Ember Nights are not calendar corrections but scars left by an ancient Ascendant war.
- River priests in the Verdant Maw maintain that floodmarks are drifting by half a day each year, though survey towers deny this.

### Adventure Hooks

- A missing treaty chest must be delivered before the last bell of Ember Night or a frontier truce collapses.
- A clock-forge in Shatterveins starts sounding a fourteenth floodmark, and three factions insist this proves different prophecies.

## NAMES IN VHAROS

Names in Vharos carry legal and social weight. Most peoples keep a birth-name, a road-name earned by deed, and a contract-name used in guild or military registers. Among Ascendants, title inflation is common and often dangerous; false claims to divine office can trigger sanctioned challenge or assassination.

### Canon Facts

- Formal records in major cities use a three-part style: birth-name, house/clan mark, and function title.
- Road-names are widely recognized in caravan law and may supersede birth-names in debt enforcement.
- Dwarven hold law in Kharad-Vul treats forged lineage marks as high treason.
- Ascendant circles commonly append epithet titles such as Ember-Crowned, Chain-Breaker, or Thorn-Blessed.

### Uncertain Reports

- It is said some old jungle cults can bind a person by secret cradle-name if spoken in a shrine court.
- Several merchant costers claim a hidden registry in West Reach tracks true names of known Ascendants for leverage.

### Adventure Hooks

- A forged clan seal appears on military orders during siege relocation, and obeying it could doom a district.
- A famed Ascendant sponsor will grant patronage only if the party retrieves proof of their suppressed birth-name.

## LANGUAGES OF VHAROS

Vharic Trade Cant serves as the practical bridge language across cities, caravans, and mixed mercenary companies. Local tongues remain strong in ritual, law, and mountain governance. In the jungle interior, speech communities shift quickly around migration, shrine authority, and predation pressure.

### Canon Facts

- Vharic Trade Cant is the default language for contracts, caravan logs, and inter-city diplomacy.
- Khardun (KHAR-dun) remains the ceremonial and legal tongue of Kharad-Vul archives.
- Lowland river settlements use multiple creoles that blend trade cant, old temple dialects, and beast-sign shorthand.
- Ascendant circles maintain coded liturgies called spark-scripts for oath exchange and power doctrine.

### Uncertain Reports

- Scribes disagree whether spark-script is merely ciphered cant or a true metaphysical language that alters vow-binding.
- Hunters speak of a whistle-code in the Verdant Maw that only works when answered by canopy predators.

### Adventure Hooks

- A treaty tablet discovered in a drowned ruin contains mixed Khardun and spark-script clauses no current court can fully read.
- A caravan route goes dark after scouts mistranslate river-creole warning posts that mark seasonal kill-zones.

## CURRENCY AND VALUE

Coin exists in Vharos, but value follows survivability. A chest of silver means little where roads are closed, escorts are dead, or clean water is scarce. Wealth is therefore counted in three ledgers: minted coin, route-claims, and salvage rights.

### Canon Facts

- Common coinage uses iron slivers, silver crowns, and gold suns, with regional mint marks affecting trust.
- Caravan bonds and salvage warrants are routinely traded as near-currency in mountain markets.
- Kharad-Vul siege protocol allows emergency requisition paid in future forge shares rather than immediate coin.
- Relic fragments from pre-collapse ruins can settle major debts if authenticated by a recognized guild examiner.

### Uncertain Reports

- Some quartermasters insist the Cinder Concord has begun minting heat-marked counterfeit suns that pass most roadside checks.
- There are persistent claims of an Underdark broker market accepting only blood-oaths and memory tithes.

### Adventure Hooks

- A convoy carrying warrant seals vanishes, freezing legal trade across three passes unless replacements are found.
- A noble house offers impossible rates for one crate of relic glass, suggesting the cargo is not what the manifest states.

## LAW, DEBT, AND JUSTICE

Law in Vharos is hard-edged and practical. Communities that cannot enforce contracts collapse into raid economies, so courts prioritize oath clarity, convoy duty, ration order, and siege discipline. Mercy exists, but it is usually procedural rather than sentimental.

### Canon Facts

- Most city charters enforce debt by labor term, convoy service, or guild seizure before imprisonment.
- Oathbreaking during declared siege is treated as a civic crime above theft and often above manslaughter.
- Trial customs differ by region, but written witness logs and bell-time stamps are widely accepted evidence.
- Ascendants are not above civil law, yet many polities require specialized tribunals when divine manifestations are involved.

### Uncertain Reports

- It is whispered that certain shrine courts can void contracts if a petitioner proves coercion by Ascendant aura.
- Judges in two frontier towns are rumored to consult an anonymous oracle ledger before issuing death warrants.

### Adventure Hooks

- The party must escort a condemned quartermaster to appellate court before a bell deadline while multiple factions seek to silence him.
- A lawful debt auction includes seizure of a village's rain cistern, and revolt becomes likely by next chain.

## TRADE, ROUTES, AND CARAVANS

Trade is the bloodstream of Vharos and the chief cause of its wars. Routes are seasonal, heavily negotiated, and often measured by casualty memory as much as distance. Caravans operate as armed institutions, carrying not only goods but treaties, intelligence, and religious obligations.

### Canon Facts

- Major routes are classified as stone-line, ash-line, or green-line based on infrastructure and hazard profile.
- Licensed caravan masters can invoke temporary way-law to settle disputes while in transit.
- Merchant costers maintain fortified relay yards where rations, beasts, and escort contracts are rotated by chain.
- Control of pass chokepoints near Kharad-Vul determines grain pricing across multiple regions.

### Uncertain Reports

- Teamsters say an uncharted green-line opens once per floodmark through the Verdant Maw and leads to untouched vault ruins.
- Some captains claim Hollow Synod agents intentionally seed false waystones to redirect rival caravans into kill corridors.

### Adventure Hooks

- A high-value medicine caravan can only pass if the party negotiates safe conduct between two feuding escort houses.
- Survey beacons on a stone-line begin broadcasting old military codes, drawing scavengers and siege refugees alike.

## FESTIVALS AND HOLY DAYS

Despite constant peril, Vharos keeps festivals with fierce discipline. Holy days are not luxuries; they reset social obligations, honor the dead, and publicly reaffirm who has authority to command in crisis. Most celebrations combine market truce, oath renewal, and controlled displays of martial or divine strength.

### Canon Facts

- Ember Nights close the civil year and are used for amnesty petitions, guild audits, and vow renewal.
- The Rainfast Rite marks the start of peak storm season, with mandatory inspections of cisterns, roofs, and ward stones.
- Chainbreak Vigil commemorates major siege escapes, especially among dwarven and mixed hold communities.
- Ascendant households are expected to sponsor public protections on holy days, such as ward-lighting or ration grants.

### Uncertain Reports

- Some elders maintain that skipping Chainbreak Vigil in a hold-city invites Underdark incursions within the next floodmark.
- There are tales of a hidden festival called the Silent Bloom where rival cults exchange prisoners under truce.

### Adventure Hooks

- During Ember Night, a public oath renewal is interrupted by a claimant bearing proof that a revered war hero swore to the enemy.
- Rainfast inspections uncover a sealed shrine chamber beneath a cistern, forcing civic leaders to choose secrecy or panic.

## GAME INFORMATION

Use this section as a table-facing baseline for travel, downtime, and faction pressure in Vharos.

- **Calendar pressure:** Treat one chain (10 days) as the default unit for downtime projects, legal deadlines, and faction clock advancement.
- **Route risk:** For overland movement outside secured stone-lines, require one group navigation check per travel day; on failure, apply supply loss, hazard encounter, or delay.
- **Contract stakes:** Breaking sworn agreements in public record should trigger social and legal consequences before combat consequences.
- **Ascendant visibility:** Each overt divine display in a populated zone increases local attention from factions, cults, and recruiters.
- **Festival windows:** Holy days are ideal timing for diplomacy, theft, coup attempts, or public revelation scenes due to concentrated leadership presence.
- **Siege tie-in:** References to Kharad-Vul should remain active in rumor traffic and market pricing until the table resolves the city's fate.
