# Session One (Detailed): The Breaking of Kharad-Vul

## Session Snapshot

- **System:** D&D 5e-compatible, Ascendency Wars
- **Recommended level:** 1 (milestone to 2 at end)
- **Expected runtime:** 4-5 hours
- **Session type:** Escape thriller with moral pressure and civic collapse
- **Core promise:** The party survives the first night of the siege, chooses who to save, and awakens Divine Cores in crisis

## Canon Anchors

This detailed version expands the established sequence:

1. Chain & Cask muster
2. Lower Forges alarm
3. Chainwell Gate breach
4. Shrine of the Nine Rivets triage
5. Crownflame ignition (awakening)
6. Ash Lift escape

Primary named NPCs:

- Captain Brakka Ironveil
- Sister Halea Ashmantle
- Liftmaster Odrin Slatehand
- Vell "Three-Tallies" Marr

## The Invading Force: The Hollow Synod

This is not a random monster rush. The force attacking Kharad-Vul is the Hollow Synod: a disciplined Underscar cult-state running a coordinated harvest operation under siege conditions.

### What They Are

- **Structure:** Hierarchical ritual theocracy with trained cells
- **Identity:** Underscar theocrats and flesh-crafters
- **Doctrine:** Divine sparks should be forcibly merged into a single perfected host
- **Campaign role:** Cataclysmic antagonist faction driving the opening arc

### What They Want in Session One

- Collapse urban order in Kharad-Vul
- Capture rather than simply kill high-value targets
- Establish ritual footholds and beacon lines for follow-on waves
- Exploit evacuation chaos to increase abduction volume

### How They Fight

- **Layered ambushes:** Strike from multiple elevations and side fissures
- **Kill-zone control:** Force defenders into narrow lanes and chokepoints
- **Attrition pressure:** Win by exhaustion, panic, and logistical overload
- **Signal warfare:** Leverage forged or disrupted civic signals to misdirect defenders
- **Ritual reinforcement:** Use prepared nodes/beacons to pull in timed support

### Why They Are So Dangerous Here

- They know the Shatterveins tunnel network and can rewrite approach routes through cave-ins, excavation, and false wayfinding
- Their battlefield edge is timing and confusion, not raw numbers alone
- They deliberately target the city's lift, bell, and corridor systems so panic multiplies faster than casualties
- Every major delay the party allows can plausibly translate to civilian captures, not just tactical losses

### GM Usage Notes

- Portray Synod units as purposeful and coordinated, not feral
- Keep "abduction under pressure" visible in at least one beat per major scene
- Reward players who disrupt ritual infrastructure with immediate tactical relief (fewer reinforcements, cleaner routes, calmer crowds)

### Synod Species Composition (Session-One Baseline)

Use this as the default field composition for the Hollow Synod assault on Kharad-Vul.

#### Core Species Mix

- **Duergar (Ashcast Cohorts):** Heavy infantry, breach engineers, shield-wall pushers, chain-sabotage teams
- **Drow/Underscar Elves (Choir Blades):** Officers, scouts, signal interceptors, ritual coordinators
- **Grimlocks and Tunnel Clans (Blindrunners):** Shock skirmishers, flankers, scent-track abductors
- **Troglodyte Converts (Sootscale Packs):** Close-quarters swarms used to overload chokepoints
- **Thrall-Cultists (Mixed Captive Ancestries):** Expendable abductors, porters, and ritual labor
- **Fleshcrafted Synod Elites (Harvest Wardens):** Rare elite enforcers deployed at key objectives

#### Suggested Battlefield Ratio

For every 10 visible Synod combatants in a scene:

- 4 Duergar (frontline and breaching)
- 2 Drow/Underscar Elves (command and control)
- 2 Grimlocks or Troglodyte Converts (flanking pressure)
- 1 Thrall-Cultist handler team (capture operations)
- 1 wildcard specialist (ritual adept, sapper, or Harvest Warden)

#### Scene-Specific Deployment Notes

- **Scene 2 (Lower Forges):** Duergar + Troglodyte pressure, limited command presence
- **Scene 3 (Chainwell Gate):** Full combined-arms push with officers and signal sabotage
- **Scene 4 (Shrine Triage):** Thrall-cultists and abductors probing weak corridors
- **Scene 6 (Ash Lift):** Sappers and ranged specialists targeting chain systems

#### Identity and Tone Guidelines

- Keep armor, tools, and ritual marks visually unified across species
- Emphasize doctrine over biology: they act like one machine with many parts
- Reserve Harvest Wardens for major spikes so they stay frightening and rare
- If you want less "standard Underdark," swap one core species for local tunnel-adapted mutants while keeping roles intact

## GM Prep (10 Minutes)

Before play, decide:

- Which NPC has prior ties to each PC
- One personal stake each PC has in Kharad-Vul (family, debt, oath, rival)
- Which faction first notices each PC by session end

Prepare these tracks on paper:

- **Breach Clock (0-6)**
- **Crowd Panic (Calm/Agitated/Breaking)**
- **Bell Code Integrity (Stable/Compromised/Forged)**

### Breach Clock Beats

- **2:** Ember Market panic wave; movement slows in public lanes
- **4:** District blackout and contradictory bell calls
- **6:** Chainwell Gate collapse; mandatory route change and severe casualties

Advance the Breach Clock by 1 when:

- The party takes a major delay under active threat
- A key objective fails loudly
- The group triggers chaotic movement in crowded zones

## Running Tone

Use these sensory motifs repeatedly:

- Hammer-echo bells, chain thunder, forge glare, ash in the lungs
- Vertical movement pressure: stairs, lifts, choke terraces
- Law versus survival: Bell Code obedience against expedient cruelty

### Bell Code at the Table

Reward compliance with the city alarm code:

- Following Bell Code grants cooperation, cleaner lanes, and authority with civilians
- Breaking code creates social friction, legal threat, or blocked access

## Scene 1: Chain & Cask Muster (30-45 min)

**Location:** Chainwell Ward tavern (crowded liftworker house)  
**Goal:** Start in social pressure, establish rumors, then trigger crisis

### Opening Read-Aloud

The Chain & Cask shakes with second-bell laughter and tankard thuds, until all at once the room goes still. Lift crews in soot-black aprons stare toward the rafters as the bells stutter out the wrong pattern. A heartbeat later, stone dust rains from the ceiling, and somewhere below the floorboards, the mountain groans.

### What Happens

- Let each PC do one short intro action (talk, gamble, read the room, pray, boast)
- Seed rumors:
  - Gate failures are sabotage, not fatigue
  - Forged lift orders carry Odrin's seal
  - Concord brokers are buying "priority" evacuation slots
- Trigger the first shockwave and crowd rush

### Skill Opportunities (suggested)

- **Insight DC 12:** Spot who is truly afraid vs opportunistically lying
- **Persuasion/Intimidation DC 13:** Get a clear answer in the chaos
- **Perception DC 12:** Notice armed strangers already moving with purpose

### Scene Choice

The party must pick a first response:

- Rally with Brakka's fallback line toward Lower Forges
- Chase a fast-exit rumor tied to Vell's intermediaries

Either path converges later, but this decision changes first impressions.

## Scene 2: Lower Forges Alarm Line (40-55 min)

**Location:** Heat-blasted terraces, chain brakes, smoke-choked lanes  
**Goal:** Introduce tactical danger plus civilian protection pressure

### Setup

Brakka is organizing a rear guard and triage corridor under falling debris. She barks orders, marks survivors, and cuts down argument time to seconds.

### Encounter Structure (choose 2 or run all 3 quickly)

1. **Hazard: Chain Brake Failure**
   - A loaded platform starts to slip above refugees
   - Resolve as a 3-success skill challenge before 2 failures
   - Good skills: Athletics, Smith's Tools, Thieves' Tools, Arcana, Guidance-style support
2. **Skirmish: Underscar Probers**
   - 1 sturdy brute + 4-6 fast raiders (reskin low-level creatures)
   - Objective is hold-and-evacuate, not wipe everything
3. **Command Dilemma**
   - Brakka orders sealing a stair with civilians still below unless a safer detour is found now
   - The party can obey, delay, or split to open a rescue lane

### Suggested DCs

- **Easy:** 11
- **Standard:** 13
- **Hard under smoke/debris:** 15

### Consequences

- Success: Brakka grants route authority token (one future militia advantage)
- Failure/Delay: Breach Clock +1 and Crowd Panic worsens one step

## Scene 3: Chainwell Gate Breach (45-60 min)

**Location:** Gate district, cistern bridges, defensive barricades  
**Goal:** Show citywide failure and force public, visible choices

### Read-Aloud

At Chainwell Gate the line has already broken. Barricades that should face the tunnels are burning from behind, and the bells overhead shout one order while runners scream another. Through smoke and falling sparks, you see militia pulling families across a cistern bridge while something with iron hooks tears into the rear ranks.

### Setpiece Options (pick one primary, one secondary)

- **Bridge Hold:** Defend evacuees for 3 rounds
- **Chain Cut Decision:** Drop a bridge segment to halt pursuers at grave cost
- **Bell Runner Recovery:** Extract wounded signal-runner carrying proof of forged bell plates

### Dynamic Complications

- Concord claim-team attempts to enforce paid priority
- False Crown order redirects defenders off the real breach axis
- Visibility collapses; ranged pressure increases from ledges

### Mechanical Notes

- Use difficult terrain in smoke zones
- Add a morale timer (if no visible heroics in 2 rounds, panic escalates)
- If Bell Code is respected publicly, civilians self-organize and reduce obstruction

If Breach Clock reaches 4 here, trigger blackout immediately.

## Scene 4: Shrine of the Nine Rivets Triage (30-45 min)

**Location:** Shrine halls converted to emergency treatment lanes  
**Goal:** Moral center of session; make choices matter socially

Sister Halea runs triage with near-supernatural calm. She does not ask for speeches; she asks for corridors and time.

### Triage Demands (run as parallel urgent tasks)

Choose 2-3:

- Escort wounded children to a valid lift queue
- Recover medicine crates from a collapsed terrace
- Remove armed looters from supply lane without collapsing order
- Stabilize a burned foreman who knows where forged seals came from

### Social Pressure

- Halea opposes cruelty, even efficient cruelty
- If party acts brutally in public, she denounces them and trust drops
- If party protects noncombatants at cost, she grants sanctuary credentials and witness intel

### Suggested Checks

- **Medicine DC 12/14:** Stabilize under pressure
- **Athletics DC 13:** Clear rubble quickly
- **Persuasion DC 13 or Authority Token:** Move crowds without stampede
- **Religion/History DC 12:** Invoke shrine protocol to gain compliance

## Scene 5: Crownflame Ignition (20-30 min)

**Location:** Open shaft terraces under red aurora glow  
**Goal:** Divine Core awakening tied to sacrifice and survival

### Trigger

A collapse and surge occur at once: one lane can be held, one can be saved, one can be abandoned. As players commit, Crownflame red light cuts through ash and each core responds.

### Awakening Procedure

For each PC:

1. Ask: "What impossible pressure are you answering right now?"
2. Give a brief sensory flare (heat, chain-song, mirrored shadow, storm crackle, etc.)
3. Grant one **Awakened Affinity Expression** usable once this scene

Keep expressions bounded:

- Prevent/reduce one hit
- Traverse one impossible gap safely
- Briefly control one key enemy
- Stabilize or shield one ally plus nearby civilian

No permanent major buffs yet; this is identity ignition, not full power spike.

---

## Scene 6: Ash Lift Escape (35-50 min)

**Location:** Emergency ascent towers and exposed lift platforms  
**Goal:** Final extraction under sabotage and overcapacity panic

Odrin controls surviving lift rotations. Forged orders create lethal jams. Vell offers a "clean lane" that costs future leverage.

### Final Route Choice

- **Odrin Route:** lawful, crowded, technically safer; slower
- **Vell Route:** faster and cleaner for selected evacuees; adds debt
- **Brakka Rear Guard Route:** most dangerous; rescues stranded fighters/civilians

### Final Encounter Recipe

Run as 3 escalating beats:

1. **Boarding chaos:** crowd control under attack
2. **Mid-ascent sabotage:** chain lock tampered, platform tilts
3. **Shaft gauntlet:** ranged foes from ledges while panic threatens overload

### Success Condition

The party and a meaningful civilian group reach upper terraces.

### Partial Success

Party escapes, but one ally group is lost, captured, or stranded.

### Failure State (still playable)

Platform crash-lands on intermediate tier; session ends with desperate regroup and enemy encroachment.

## NPC Playbook (Quick Reference)

### Captain Brakka Ironveil

- Voice: clipped commands, no wasted words
- Wants: orderly survival, defensible withdrawals, proof of sabotage
- Offers: militia backing, route authority, hard truth
- Break point: sentimental delay that kills lines

### Sister Halea Ashmantle

- Voice: quiet, absolute, morally anchored
- Wants: life-first triage and corridor discipline
- Offers: stabilization, refuge access, witness intelligence
- Break point: public cruelty framed as necessity

### Liftmaster Odrin Slatehand

- Voice: technical, impatient, exact
- Wants: obeyed load limits and clean chain orders
- Offers: priority ascent windows, service-shaft routes
- Break point: anyone overriding engineering reality

### Vell "Three-Tallies" Marr

- Voice: smooth, amused, always pricing
- Wants: leverage and future influence
- Offers: shortcuts, papers, shadow lanes
- Break point: unpaid debt or humiliation in public

## Scaling Guide

- **Smaller group (3 PCs):** reduce enemy count, keep hazards, tighten moral choices
- **Larger group (5-6 PCs):** add split objectives and layered vertical threats
- **Low combat table:** emphasize hazards, social authority, and rescue clocks
- **High combat table:** keep objectives non-lethal (hold lanes, protect evacuees, extract runners)

---

## Fast GM Checklist

- Open with wrong bell pattern and tavern shockwave
- Advance Breach Clock when time/control is lost
- Put civilians in motion every scene
- Make at least one irreversible choice visible by Scene 3
- Trigger awakening only at true no-win pressure
- End with survival, consequence, and clear next hook
