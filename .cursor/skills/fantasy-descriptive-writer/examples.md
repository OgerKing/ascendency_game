# Fantasy Descriptive Writer Examples

Use these as quick prompt starters with the `fantasy-descriptive-writer` skill.

## Universal Prompt Frame

Copy and fill:

```text
Write a [length] fantasy description of [subject].
Tone: [grim | wondrous | eerie | sacred | playful | courtly].
POV: [neutral narrator | close third | first person chronicler].
Include: [3 concrete sensory details], [1 cultural/lore trace], [1 tension element].
Avoid: generic filler and cliches.
```

## Realms-Like Prompt Frame

Use this when you want classic setting-guide cadence:

```text
Write this in a classic 1e/2e-era setting-guide voice inspired by early Forgotten Realms material.
Keep it original (no copied phrasing), but match the cadence: lore-dense, practical, and evocative.
Subject: [city | region | frontier road | ruin | noble house | temple network].
Length: [short entry | 2-4 paragraphs].
Include: geography, power groups, trade/livelihood, notable hazards, and 2 adventure hooks.
End with a rumor, warning, or unresolved tension.
```

## Canonical Entry Frame (Direct Style Match)

```text
Write an original setting entry using this exact section structure:

AT A GLANCE:
[2-4 lines: identity, location, strategic role]

CHRONICLER'S NOTES:
[2-3 paragraphs: history, power groups, local tensions, notable names with pronunciation]

GAME INFORMATION:
- [travel hazard]
- [faction leverage point]
- [1-3 adventure hooks]
- [optional quick stat block if requested]

Style target: classic late-80s Realms boxed-set cadence.
Do not copy phrases from any published text.
```

## Location Prompts

### Grimdark Border Keep

```text
Write a 170-word description of a storm-battered border keep at dusk.
Tone: grim and exhausted, but not hopeless.
POV: neutral narrator.
Include wet stone textures, distant iron sounds, and signs of old sieges.
Add one omen at the end.
```

### High Fantasy Sky City

```text
Write a cinematic 320-word passage describing a sky city built around floating crystal roots.
Tone: wondrous with hints of political tension.
POV: close third from a first-time visitor.
Include air pressure sensations, bell-chime acoustics, and ceremonial iconography.
Close on a quiet but unsettling detail.
```

### Realms-Style City Entry

```text
Write a 3-paragraph setting entry for the port city of Marath Cove.
Style target: classic early Forgotten Realms sourcebook cadence (original wording only).
Paragraph 1: geography, skyline, and first-impression atmosphere.
Paragraph 2: who holds power (merchant costers, temple interests, and one covert faction).
Paragraph 3: trade realities, local dangers, and 2 concise adventure hooks.
Keep names concrete and details gameable.
```

### Realms-Style Frontier Region

```text
Write a 4-paragraph regional gazetteer entry for the Thornwash Marches.
Style target: early setting-book tone, practical and vivid.
Cover terrain, settlements, roads/river routes, politics, faith tensions, and known threats.
Include one short "travelers say..." rumor line and finish with a hard warning.
```

### Realms-Style Deity Entry

```text
Write a deity entry in classic setting-guide style.
Format:
- Name (pronunciation)
- Titles
- Portfolio and alignment
- Symbol
- Notes paragraph

Tone: concise, game-useful, and evocative.
Include worship behavior, favored followers, and one manifestation detail.
Keep wording original.
```

### Haunted Marsh

```text
Write two short paragraphs describing a haunted marsh at dawn.
Tone: eerie and restrained.
POV: neutral narrator.
Include fog behavior, insect silence, and one relic from a forgotten pilgrimage route.
End with a question that implies danger.
```

### Cozy Tavern Variant

```text
Write a 140-word tavern description in a river trade town.
Tone: warm, lively, and slightly rough-edged.
POV: first person traveler.
Include smells, overlapping voices, and one local custom that reveals social hierarchy.
```

## Character Prompts

### Weathered Knight

```text
Describe a retired knight-errant in 150 words.
Tone: grounded, melancholy, dignified.
Focus on physical details that imply history rather than stating it directly.
Include one object they carry and why others notice it.
```

### Young Court Mage

```text
Write a character sketch (180 words) of a young court mage masking fear with confidence.
Tone: courtly with sharp undercurrents.
POV: close third.
Include one clothing detail, one ritual habit, and one social misstep.
```

## Creature and Artifact Prompts

### Creature Introduction

```text
Introduce a nocturnal forest predator in 130 words.
Tone: tense and observational.
Avoid naming it until the final line.
Include movement pattern, hunting sign, and one unnatural sensory cue.
```

### Relic Description

```text
Describe a relic sword sealed in a shrine alcove (160 words).
Tone: sacred with latent threat.
Include material details, inscription wear, and traces of old maintenance rites.
End with implied consequence if drawn.
```

## Culture and Lore Fragment Prompts

### Chronicle Entry

```text
Write a lore fragment as a monastery chronicle entry (220 words) about the Year of Ash Rains.
Tone: formal, restrained, and quietly fearful.
Include one contradiction between official record and physical evidence.
```

### Tavern Gossip

```text
Write a tavern gossip excerpt (120 words) about a missing tax barge.
Tone: playful, suspicious, and local.
Include one proverb and one wrong detail the speaker believes is true.
```

## Rewrite Prompts

### Make Generic Text Specific

```text
Rewrite the following fantasy paragraph to be more vivid and specific while preserving all lore facts.
Keep length between 120 and 160 words.
Replace abstractions with concrete sensory cues and stronger verbs.
After the rewrite, provide 3 bullet notes describing what improved.

[PASTE SOURCE TEXT]
```

### Tone Shift Without Lore Drift

```text
Rewrite this passage from wondrous tone to ominous tone.
Do not change names, timeline facts, or location details.
Keep approximately the same length.
Afterward, provide 2 notes on the most important tone changes.

[PASTE SOURCE TEXT]
```

## Fast Defaults

Use these when user intent is brief:

- "Describe [place] in one vivid paragraph, with one sensory hook and one subtle threat."
- "Write a character snapshot of [name] that shows status and hidden motive in under 140 words."
- "Make this lore text less generic and more grounded in physical detail."
